Chase, we want freedom

JPMorgan Chase Bank is yanking away some consumer rights from credit card holders, but we can stop them.

Do you have one of their credit cards, like Sapphire, Slate, or United MileagePlus? Did you get a letter or email from them about “Changes to your Account”? Then this is important.

With their “forced arbitration” clause, they’re changing the agreement so that you can’t get class-action payouts or sue them in open court if they mistreat you as a customer. For details on why this is bad, see the coverage on Boing Boing and Fast Company.

They’ve made it purposely hard to opt out of this change, by requiring you to write a physical letter, sign it and mail it. If you’re happy to DIY, here’s a form letter.

I’m lazy and hate snail mail. But I figured, it’s easier if I write a form letter, we all sign it, and then I ship them all in one go, so here it is. I reckon $3 should probably cover the signature software, printing and mailing costs.
